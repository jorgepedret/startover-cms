    FAILURE MODE ANALYSIS

    Failure Mode Analysis means to analyze possible modes of failure. This is the opposite of pessimism, which is the general view that things won't work out.

    Failure Mode Analysis is the Practice of staying acutely aware of the multitude of ways things might possible not work out in order to intelligently avoid those circumstances so that things have a better chance of working out. It is an aspect of the Boy Scout aphorism, "Thinking fast in an emergency depends on the habbit of thinking things out in advance." But in this case, you think of ways that things would fail to work out so that you can navigate around them to conserve your creation energy and time.

    A simple example of Failure Mode Analysis is noticing where the water glass is located with regards to the edge of a table while also noticing things such as pot handles or napkins or unconscious arm movements which could easily knock the glass off the table. By predicting the Failure Mode of the glass getting knocked off the table, you can make simple avoidance moves to prevent the potential failure. It costs you far less time, energy, and attention to avoid the failure than it would to clean up the mess afterwards.
