  • Are You Assuming You Have No Choice?

    Naming Your Identities

    You might think you have no other choice but to act as you do. You might create this illusion for yourself by thinking you have an identity that is real, permanent, solid and cannot shift, and which has a fixed set of “needs.” But this is all internal politics! Whichever “identity” happens to be in power in this moment gets to determine which “needs” are “real” and must be met.

    To wreck the Identification Game, do the experiment of “Naming the Identities.”

     

    “Naming” is a powerful alchemical act. By giving a name to something that did not have a name before, you change the thing from invisible to visible, from subjective to objective, from unconscious to conscious, from background to foreground. By clearly naming an identity, you bring people’s attention to the option of shifting to a different identity.


    By changing the identity of the moment, you change the “needs” of the moment. For example: You can name your own identities in order to catch yourself in the act. You may identify characters such as The Whiner, The Judge, The Complainer, The Victim, The Glutton, The Perfectionist, The Head Chopper, The Sneak, The Miser, and so on.


    Or, you can use naming to open doorways through which you can then step, and thus shift your identity in that moment. One way to open a doorway is to say something that the new character might say, such as: “Enter The Dragon”; “May I present The Goddess”; “Jamison at your service, Madam”; “Which way to Kilimanjaro?”; “Choose your weapon!”; “What have we here, Sherlock?”; “Has anyone seen my Ferrari keys?”
    You can also open doorways through which to shift identity without saying anything.


    For example, if you are going to bed with your partner at night and you feel the same old evening pattern arising, shift identity. For example, change the timing of your entry into the room. Take a different physical posture – crawl in, strut in, limp in, come in backwards, come in with your eyes closed. Use a different breathing pattern. Put on or take off different pieces of clothing. Change your mood. Beat up the teddy bear. Sing. If, while you are practicing shifting identities, your partner thinks that you have gone nuts, tell them that before now you were only pretending not to be nuts, and that you think they will get used to it.


    The idea is to reveal yourself, rather than waiting around for your partner to reveal themselves first. It is crucial to remember that revealing yourself does not involve complaining, gossiping, blaming, or talking about other people. Revealing yourself is about getting present and letting yourself be authentically known. You may not be able to manage this the first time you try it, so plan to keep experimenting.

    An Exploration by Israel Kairos

    This is the first time my hands have written a website.

    I do not need to have written a website before to be a Website Writer. Right now, I declare: I AM a Website Writer.

     

    That is what shifting identity is all about.

     

    When about to take a new actions, most human beings are faced with two barriers.

    Barrier #1: Learning Something New.

     

    Barrier #2: Your Box's Beliefs that tells you what you are and what you are not or what you can and what you cannot do.

     

    Human beings are designed to learn things. As a child, you are faced with a whole world of new things. You quickly adapt. You learn. But Learning seems to become much slower as you grow older… even though you have more knowledge, tools, and insight. Why?

    What I am discovering is that at some point in our lives, we limit ourselves to what we believe we know how to do.

     

    For example, I have experienced many time - and it is still extremely curious - that in our childhood, we make decisions that change the way we relate to the world, and then we completely forget these decisions. We put them in drawers at the bottom of our unconscious and we never return to check if these decisions still make sense.

     

    These decisions have shaped who you think you are, what you think you know or do not know, what you think you are able to do or not do. You might have heard from your parents: "You are very good at this." or "You are not good for that.", and then you go on living listening to those voices in your head telling us what you can and cannot do.

     

    Shifting Identity is an experiment. An invitation. I don't ask you to believe me. I invite you to try it. Experience what else is possible when you remove Barrier #2 from your Learning process. What happens to your learning process when the only challenge you face is venturing into something new? Instead of fighting a big battle against what you think you can and what you think you can't do.


    Being very direct I would say this: Shifting Identity is declaring what you ARE in a small NOW.

    As you are the space you inhabit, declaring what you are is also setting the context of your own space. Context defines space, and space defines what is possible. Shifting Identity defines what is possible for you.

     

    Do all the E.H.P.s that are necessary for you to Shift of Identity so that you can become the necessity asks of you and what the E.C.C.O. invites you, without ceasing to be You, because what your Being is has nothing to do with what you can or cannot do.

  • You Have Parts

    Human Beings have parts... many parts. We don't know when we shift from one identity to the next, but if the phone rings and it is our boss, our mother, or our child, the person we were just speaking with won't know who just showed up. And when we shift back to being 'ourselves' with our friend, we won't even know we shifted. You can make this whole shifting thing conscious. Then things start getting interesting.

    We start again with the eternal question, “Who am I?”

     

    You have now, and have always had, the complete freedom to choose who you are being. It is true that you have been raised in certain circumstances and in a certain culture and time. It is true that there exist powerful forces to try to sway your decisions in one direction or another about who to be. But those circumstances and forces are now, and always have been, outside of you, incidental, and not causative. The final decision – even down to the finest minutiae of your quality of being – has always been yours alone.

     

    There are of course consequences to the decisions you make. Your assumptions about these consequences make it seem like you actually have no choice and that you are squeezed into our way of being like warm plastic is squeezed into a steel mold. Cultural “being shaping” machinery is indeed powerful, as is family tradition. That is why the vast majority of us surrender our capacity to shape our own being to fit the illusion that it was never our decision in the first place. Nevertheless, the “vast majority” does not have to include you.

     

    “Who am I?” is one of the first questions we are driven to answer. Once you find workable answers you usually don’t change them for the rest of your life. You don’t even wonder if your answers are the most useful or interesting. You would assume yours answers are true and consider the job finished. Once you have built your Box, you forget that your Box is something that was built. Unconsciously, you know that if you reconsider the “Who am I?” question it could drop us back into a liquid state – one that you assumed you had permanently left behind. The Box is quick to produce the terse comment, “Been there. Done that.”

     

    However . . . nonetheless . . . notwithstanding . . . you still have the right to change your mind about who you are. That has not been made illegal. After all, your Box is yours to play with and to make into whatever you want. That is why you are reading this book – to play with other possible shapes for your Box.

     

    Consciously changing your Box is an option that you may tend to ignore if you have not acquired a taste for being in flux. Having the option to take on any identity, in any moment, is the same as having no identity at all. Those in-between times of making a new decision often seem bothersome – they are indefinite; we entered them after leaving behind 'the way it was' and before having in place a new 'the way it is'.
