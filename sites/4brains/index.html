  • YOU HAVE 4 BRAINS

    There are four types of brain and you have all four.

    So does everyone else.

    One of your brains is 'bigger' than the other. Your 'bigger' brain is the one you are most identified with, the one you use the most in your interactions with the world.

    From experience, it is possible to balance more equally the 'size' of all your four brains through initiatory and evolutionary processes.

    You don't interact with the world as it is, you interact with the world through the maps of the world. One of those maps is which brain you use to interact with the world.

    Each brain has a specific perspective, a specific way of interacting, a specific way of questioning, a specific learning process, etc...

    Each brain has immense intelligence.

    A team need all four brains in a somewhat balanced amount.

    A village functions properly when the four brains are balanced in the community.

    Green Brain

    The green brain is the reptilian brain. 'Our' first brain. The first brain that was developed in the animals that became your ancestors. The green brain is about the past, about connection, about inclusion and about family. The green brain is slower. A 'green brain' person (someone with a predominant green brain) would need to know where you come from, who your parents are, how many brothers and sisters you have and how you arrived where you are to feel connected to you. They would need to know all of this before they could do business with you, or start a project, or decide about where to have lunch.

    Green brains are wonderful connectors.

    Red Brain

    The red brain is the mammalian brain. 'Our' second brain. The brain that developed on top of the reptilian brain. The red brain is about action, about 'me, me, me', about the present and about what's next. A 'red brain' person cannot sit for a long time, they are not interested in discussion, they want action. Their now is small, they don't think about the past, nor about the long term future. They will ask 'what next?' or simply move.

    In a team, red brains bring dynamism and movement.

    Blue Brain

    The blue brain is the cortex. 'Our' third brain. The brain that developed after the red brain. The blue brain is about planning, about the future, about concepts and purposes, about making decisions and strategies. A 'blue brain' person needs to know about what, when, and how. A blue brain will discuss strategy and plan before they can take action. Blue brains need to understand, and to share similar concepts to communicate.

    Blue brains are great planners and guardians.

    Yellow Brain

    The Yellow Brain is in the center of our brain. It's the latest brain to develop. The yellow brain is timeless and has no time, no past and no future. The yellow brain has vision and is holistic. It is about the big picture. The yellow brain is also slower than the red and blue brain. The yellow brain is not in a hurry because it doesn't relate to time in a linear way, but in a circular way. 'Yellow brain' people have meta-conversations. Yellow brains ask about purpose and the vision of the project or the proposal.

    In a team, yellow brains bring perspective and slowness.

    YOU HAVE 4 BRAINS

    Matrix Code 4BRAINSx.01

    We all have 4 brains. And in each of us, one of the 4 brains is bigger than the others. Because it is bigger, it has greater processing power and it is the one that works the most for us. To give an example, it's like you have 4 pairs of shoes in your closet and you always put on the same ones. That's not good or bad and maybe there are more elegant ways.

    Find a person to hold space for your 4 brains. This experiment also works online.

    The job of the spaceholder is to interview each of your 4 brains for 8 minutes. The spaceholder doesn't necessarily need to know more details about the 4 brains, it's enough that you know more details. The spaceholder only needs to know that he is talking to one of your 4 brains and what color it is.

    So the spaceholder asks and you answer as one of these brains. The spaceholder interviews all 4 of your brains in turn. (If you do it online, record it).

    The spaceholder should ask questions like:

    - How are you doing?

    - What is working well with you and NAME right now?

    - What is not working?

    - What are you feeling? What do you need?

    - As a green/red/blue/yellow brain, how are you interacting with the world, with people?

    TIME LAPSE

    Matrix Code 4BRAINSx.02

    Your day has 24 hours. Let's say that you sleep 8 hours of it, then there are 16 hours left in which you are awake.

    For this experiment, you are to do everything at 1.5 times the normal speed three times a day for a period of 15 minutes. For 15 minutes you move 1.5 times faster, you speak 1.5 times faster, you eat, walk, work 1.5 times faster.

    So set your alarm clock early in the morning so you don't forget. When it rings, the 15 minutes start. Set the timer for that.

    Because this is extremely exciting and unfamiliar, I recommend that you spread out the three time slots over the entire span of your waking hours. On Day 1, start with 15 minutes when you're alone.

    LEAVE THE HIGHWAY, ENTER THE JUNGLE

    Matrix Code 4BRAINSx.04

     

    Tracks form in our brains over the years that can become very deep, making it increasingly difficult to leave them. These are our habits. When we drive on the highway of our habits, we're pretty darn fast. We know how to do it without thinking about it and we speed along in our habits.

    This experiment makes you take the next exit. Into the jungle. You don't know anything anymore. (You can do the following experiments one after the other, at the same time, or not at all).

     

    - If you are right-handed, do everything with your left hand for a week (eat, write, brush your teeth, open the door, hold the bottle, pick your nose, etc.).

    - Invite friends over for dinner: sit on the floor and eat without cutlery. Chew each bite 40 times.

    - Rearrange your apartment/room. Switch bedrooms and living rooms.

    - Sleep outside for a few days.

    - Rotate your sleeping position in bed: where your feet usually are, your head is now. Sleep in this position until you get used to it. Then change it again.

    - Shower only with cold water.

    - Do not enter a supermarket for a whole month. Shop somewhere else.

    - Wear your parting on the other side. Keep doing this until you get used to it.

    - Clasp your hands in front of your chest. How do you do this? Which hand is down, where are the fingers? Now switch it around completely once, do it mirror image in the unfamiliar way. Do it the new way until you get used to it. Then change something and find a new variation.

    WHATS NOW?

    Matrix Code 4BRAINSx.06

    Set your watch ahead by 10 minutes. (Don't have a watch? Find ways to get a watch for a week).

    That's just the first part of the experiment. Now, instead of thinking about what's about to happen, here's what you do:

    - Get up from your chair, turn around, and take a step back. You're moving from "Whats next?" to "Whats now?" Notice what is now. What are you feeling right now? Write the results in your BEEP!Book.

    Do the first part of the experiment for a whole week. Do the second part of the experiment whenever you are given a cushion of time.
