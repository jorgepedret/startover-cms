          FEAR CLUB

        • What Is Rage Club Spaceholding?

          What is Rage Club?

          Rage Club is a radically safe learning environment for unleashing the archetypal force of Stellated Anger.

           

          The purpose of Rage Club is to grow up in relation to the feeling and emotion of Anger so you can consciously and productively use Conscious Anger's energy and information as an Adult in your daily life without being possessed or overwhelmed by it.

           

          Rage Club is both a Thoughtware Upgrade and Healing Space with regards to Anger.

          What is Rage Club Spaceholding?

          Rage Club spaceholding is that particular spaceholding where you Take A Stand for others to grow up with regards to their Anger, becoming a new and different person, someone who consciously uses Anger and relates to Conscious Anger as an Archetypal force of nature.

           

          A particular kind of spaceholding is needed for those people who are courageous enough to learn something that most people in their life have never even heard of, namely, how to own and use their Conscious Rage. People who come to Rage Club are often Edgeworkers. Rage Club is about empowering Edgeworkers.

           

          The Rage Club Spaceholder must have a foot in each world:

          1. One foot in the ordinary world where a first-time Rage-Clubber stands
          2. One foot in the extraordinary world where Rage Club unleashes the potential of an Archetypal Force in each person's daily life. 

          This kind of Double Spaceholding - or 'bridge spaceholding' - can best be learn by practicing it in the presence of Rage Club Spaceholder Trainer who can already do this and indicate to you what is actually going on and why they do (or do not do...) the things they do (or do not do...).

          What is Rage Club Spaceholder Training?

          During Rage Club Spaceholder Training, you learn to:

          • How to Set Context - in particular the Extraordinary Context - of Rage Club with clarity, power and lightness.

          • How to face Gremlin resistance and attack.

          • How to distinguish when Emotional Healing Processes (EHP) are needed, and how to arrange for the Team to do them for each other.

          • How to spontaneously create exercises and experiments for the matrix of your participants.

          • How to Create Possibility for someone dealing with their modern-culture life while also dealing with being in Rage Club.

          • How to match the exercises you give to the next steps of each participants.

          • How to 'roll the energy' in the space from one exercise to the next.

          • How to build a Team among the participants from the very first session.

          • How to announce and invite people into your Rage Club (meaning to Build Your Circle).

          • And more, depending on your Questions.

          A Rage Club Spaceholder Training is a space where you can realize that you are not alone on this journey of supporting other to upgrade their thoughtware to create a better future for humanity. You cannot hold the context of Rage Club alone.

           

          In the Rage Club Spaceholder Training you may find a new home.

        • Rage Club Formats

           

           
          Listed here are the usual formats used by Rage Club spaceholders around the world:
          • Introduction to Rage Club. An Introduction is a one-time, 2-hour (or longer) space. Introduction to Rage Club is, as its name indicates, an introduction to the territory and resource of Anger. Most participants won’t ever have experienced conscious Anger in their entire lives before coming into your space. Introduction to Rage Club is a delicate and not superficial (because experiential) initiation to a new relationship to the world through a changed relation to Anger
          •  
          • Work-talk about Rage. A work-talk is a one-time, 2-hour (or longer) space. Work-talk examples are ‘Say No! Say Yes!,’ ‘Anger as a Source of Creation,’ ‘Reclaim Your Authority,’ ‘The Power of Anger,’ etc. Work-talks are different from Introductions in the sense that they focus on a particular facet of Anger. Participants in work-talks are often a mix between uninitiated and more mature Ragers.
          •  
          • Rage Club Series. Online or offline, Rage Club Series are 4, 6, 8, 10 or 12-week trainings with one 2 to 3-hour session per week. The purpose of your Introduction is to direct participants towards your Rage Club Series where they can deepen and widen their capacity to feel, express and use Anger consciously.
          •  
          • Rage Club Workshop. Usually done offline, Rage Club is a 1 to 3-day long training that meets 6 to 8 hours per day. For example, a Rage Club could start on Friday from 6.00-8.00pm, continue on Saturday from 9.00am-6.00pm with 1hour break from 1-2pm and conclude on Sunday from 9.00am-4.00pm.
          •  
          • Festivals. Rage Club works fabulously in festivals, even outdoor festivals, with large numbers of people. For large groups, the recommendation is to keep the context and explanations to a minimum and move quickly into action. Rage Club festival spaces can last from 1h to 6h.
          These formats have been tested and approved with successful results. You are welcome to experiment with your own formats.

        • RCST Prerequisites

          How to join a Rage Club Spaceholder Training?

          YOU

          • Have stellated your own anger to 100% and fully or partly stellated your own fear, sadness and joy as they come up when working with anger.

          • Can hold space.

          • Can experientially distinguish between feelings and emotions in yourself and in others.

          • Can experientially distinguish between YOUR feelings and emotions and OTHER PEOPLES' feelings and emotions.

          • Have your Gremlin by your side most of the time to not be hooked by other Gremlins.

          • Are mostly in Phase 2 of anger. In Phase 1, you learn to express anger. In Phase 2, you learn to use you anger consciously. 

          • Have personally experienced and can fully deliver the following core rage processes: twisting towel, standing rage with & without resistance, floor rage with & without resistance.

          • Have already participated in at least one Introduction to Rage Club and one 4-Week Rage Club.
