    MEANING IS NOT IN THE MIND

    In terms of memetic speaking and experiential space, the distinction is what I wanted to hear more about, to be moved into the experiential space of my inner and outer wars: what are my strategies to create inner and outer war, what am I unaware of? If you bring me into the clarity of where I am. My 'x' on the map - which is amazingly similar to your strategies, and that's why you can talk about it with such clarity - then I have the necessity to go somewhere else. How can I go somewhere else? You have the answer: Anger. I am ready to listen. You are not saying where I am going, you are only offering the space for practicing and discovering a new useful tool. What I do with it is up to me.

     

    This is because Memetic Speaking is NOT about words.

    It is not about finding words, playing with words, speaking words over there to someone else, or thinking words.

    Words exist in the mind.

    Mind is the prison.

    Last week's Memetic Speaking Spaceholding #8 was painful for me because it looked like you were looking in your mind for words, so then as a result the space was vacant, dead. There was no spaceholder. There was a mental acrobat looking for words.

    What I suggest it that Memetic Speaking is about meaning.

    Meaning happens before words, even without words.

    After there is meaning, then words can be found - if necessary - and used to build bridges so that meaning can go from one person to another. That is all words are, parts of bridges if necessary. If both people are on the same side of a bridge, no words are needed.

    Meaning has value. Words are only tools to transport meaning.

    This could be an entirely new start for you, Sir. For everyone!

    Words happen in the mind.

    Where does meaning happen? NOT IN THE MIND.

    Find out clearly where meaning happens. Figure out how to live there...

    Could we start again, please?

    CLEAR INNER NAVIGATING LEADS TO CLEAR THINKING LEADS TO CLEAR SPEAKING

    • Shift from 'I' to 'you'. When you use 'you', the distinction becomes much closer to the participant. Otherwise, they can keep it away from them - they can think " oh it is her experience, it is not mine".
    • Avoid using 'it' when you are referring to the distinction, say the distinction instead. For example, instead of saying "It can be..." keep landing the distinction in your participants matrix "Your energetic center can be in the future. Your energetic center can be in the past...") It might sound like you are repeating yourself over and over again, and that is how the distinction can go through the Box of your participants. Distinction need to be said over and over again. It makes the distinction sharper and real, present.
    • Be clear about what you mean. In Memetic Speaking you mean every word that you say. For example, when you say "my center jumps..." - no it does not, what happens is that you give your center away. That is the distinction. A jumping center is not as useful a distinction as giving your center away to land in someone Memetic structure.
    • Be very attentive when we say: "You have the power to choose". If somebody does not carry a distinction in their Being, then they DO NOT have the power to choose (which means truly taking responsibility), because they cannot even distinguish the possibility of that choice (remember when you did not know that you had a center, could you choose where it would go? No). Therefore, it is your job not to tell them they have choice BUT to provide them with the transformation that will allow them to choose.

    These hints makes your speaking meaningful and real so that the distinctions can go through your Box, and through the Box of your participant into their Being. You are not giving them an interesting story to think about, you are building new clarity into their Being.

    LANDING DISTINCTIONS IN A SPACE OR IN SOMEONE'S 5 BODIES

    The practice to develop the skill of landing distinctions (aka Memetic Speaking).

     

    Here are some offers for you to keep practicing landing distinction in 5 bodies:

    • If you speak in 'I', it makes the content of your communcation about you instead of about your participants. Your participants come to your spaces so you can give them a clear 'x' on the map, not present them with your 'x' on the map. This is why when you land distinctions, your main support is to scan your participants and tell them where they are; using the distinction that you are wanting to land. There is no judgment in giving them their 'x' on the map, it is a neutral assessment of reality.
    • Notice your own memes when you are speaking memetically. Judgements, Assumptions, Conclusions and Beliefs are memes, not very useful ones in terms of creating Extraordinary, but memes nonetheless. For example, when you started speaking about other people's relationship, you shift into a judgmental, or resentful tone, as if having an enmeshed relationship or childish reaction is bad. Does that make any sense to you that you might have the idea that not having a "perfect" relationship means that you are bad or you are a bad man, or ...? This would then be an EHP for you.
    • Holding space for transformation is not about what you want or what you don't want. It is about providing new clarity / new thoughtware. You mention about 20 different core distinctions in less than 4 minutes without deepening any of them. In reality, every one of these distinctions are crucial to creating extraordinary relationship. And simply mentioning them creates no clarity and no transformation.
    • The proposal is to choose 1 or 2 distinctions and diving into them: what does it mean to be centered? how does one give their center away to their partner? what are their benefits to giving your center away? (resentment, blame, victimhood, hatred, ...) what are the results in the relationship? what are the result in their life? (Make it painfully true. When a woman gives her center away to a man, she plays the little girl who wants to be admired by daddy.) How can you notice when you give your center away? What can you do then? And more... You could have a Rage Club only about Centering.
    • Choose the distinction to deepen that is the most relevant for you. What are you practicing or noticing in your relationship? Then you deliver this distinction, you will have flesh and juice to bring to the research.

    - do not repeat words, stalling

    - no pauses

    - steady pace, slower than usual speaking

    - statements, examples, sharings about yourself are limited, memetic speaking is not

    WORDS ARE THE BUILDING BLOCKS OF MEMES

    MATRIX CODE MEMESPEA.01

    Memes are made up of single words.

    Which words you use is no coincidence. You use the words that are part of your habitual ways of thinking. By saying things the same way over and over again, you're deepening the tracks of thinking most familiar to you and thereby making them into the mechanical structure of your Box. The words you use repeatedly indicate which memes you carry.

     

    Ask 10 people the following question: What are the three words I use most often when I talk to you?

    Write all the results on a piece of paper and put put it on your fridge so you can see the list several times a day.

     

    Additionally write down the 3 words which were mentioned most frequently on your hand for three days in a row. Observe when you use it and everytime you do, raise your hand high over your head and say: "I just used this word, because... . What I actually want to say is..."

    You might discover hints about your survival strategy and about the driving force behind. Here are some hints:

    Why do you choose this word habitually? What is so important?

    What meaning does it have for you?

    How is this word used by other people? What are they actually trying to say? What is it, that you really want to express?

    REVEAL YOUR THOUGHTWARE MEMES

    MEMESPEA.02

    Memes build thoughtware. You pick up your main memes by what you learned from your parents. You adopt the thoughtware they carry. They adopted their thoughtware from their parents. Now where did they have their thoughtware from? Exactly.

    The thoughtware currently used by the majority of people is ancient and has not received an upgrade to face the challenges at hand.

     

    In this experiment you will dust off the cobwebs of your passed down thoughtware by revealing it.

     

    Write down 10 assumptions you have about relating. Let the sentences all start with the words: "I have the assumption that... "

    Take your list to 3 members of your family. This can be your mother, your grandfather, your aunt and even your cousin. Ask them if they would be willing to look at family pictures with you and find out the memes about relating that the people in the picture carry. Have a real conversation with your family member, reveal your own assumptions about relating and ask them about theirs.

    - is the woman standing behind her husband?

    - what are the facial expressions saying?

    - who repeatedly shows up in the pictures not standing next to a certain other person?

    - what does the body language express?
