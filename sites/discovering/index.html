    SPACIAL REQUIREMENTS FOR DISCOVERING

    Discovering does not often occur in ordinary circumstances.

    For example,

    • The Space must welcome Discovering rather than defending against it.
    • Participants in the Space require a certain Attention to Notice that Discovering is happening.
    • The Space needs to contain Authentic Necessity for Discovering to be awakened.
    • Spaceholders are prepared to capture and document whatever the Discovering journey reveals.
    • The next Space is about celebrating the abundance of Discoveries.
    • A capacity to make practical use of what is Discovered. Using what is Discovered is a way of expressing gratitude and paying respects to the Source of Possibility out of which the new configuration emerged. Once the package is tossed to you it will pass you by or crash unless you participate in the exchange and embrace the package.

    'Having a conversation' and 'creating a discovery space' are two wildly different Spaces.

     

    For a Discovery Space to be born, at least one person need to 'sacrifice' themselves at the altar of being in pain about not knowing how it goes. This is the one who comes with truly authentic questions - the Discovery Listener. The Listener uses their pain about the lack of Clarity and Distinctions that truly affecting their life.     ASSUMPTIONS ABOUT DISCOVERING

    Matrix Code DISCOVER.02

    Write down all the assumptions that come up in connection with the 10 things you want to discover.

    I'll give you an example: One of the 10 things you want to discover is a perpetual motion machine. This is a non-existent device that, once set in motion, can keep moving forever without any further energy input. Your physics teacher back in school taught that a perpetual motion machine contradicts the current laws of nature and therefore it will never exist.

    One of your assumptions is that your physics teacher is right and such a device can never be discovered. So if you have this assumption, you will never ever be able to discover a perpetual motion machine.

    Even if one were right in front of you, you would not be able to see it. Assumptions can be like road blocks.

    Write down all the assumptions you have about the things you would like to discover.

    WHAT DO YOU BURN FOR?

    Matrix Code DISCOVER.07

    I have an assumption: discovery is not a linear affair. So, Christopher Columbus did not set out to discover America.
    But how does it work then? I read on this homepage that discovering can be an orthogonal path. I didn't know this word and looked up what it meant. What appealed to me is the explanation that orthogonal can mean non-redundant, non-overlapping, or even irrelevant. In other words, anything but linear.
    Example: I was reading this home page about discovery, I didn't read through it from top to bottom, but stumbled on the word orthogonal. Looking up what that is, I stumbled on the word redundancy and something inside me caught fire.

    I read a redundancy is the multiple mention of the same information. In a meeting, deadly boredom is guaranteed when that occurs: it's already been said, but not yet by everyone.

    That is one meaning of redundancy. In medical technology, we speak of redundancy when a patient has two ventilators because he would suffocate should one fail. That's redundancy that can save his life.

    I stumbled over the term orthogonal and left the linear path there. Later I turned off again: at the word redundant. I followed my own path, where my fires burned.

    Here comes an idea for an experiment to explore orthogonality:
    Start looking at the thing you want to discover. Observe yourself with a few percent of your attention, so you don't miss the little fires you start. Then follow the fire. Don't go straight ahead, linearly, but follow the fire.

    In my case, the next fire on the path would be the word redundancy.

    What is there to discover when everyone on a team says the same thing over and over for an entire evening, just in different words?

    An exciting experiment for me. Where are your fires burning?
