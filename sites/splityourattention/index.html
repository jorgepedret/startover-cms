    • The Problem With Splitting Your Attention

      It is not all peaches and cream.

      Becoming aware of what you were not aware that you were not aware of before can ruin your day.

      Your secret agreement to not notice what others do not notice permits you to graze among the Zombies as a Zombie.

      Being a sleeping Zombie may seem like the best solution to the current situation. But is it?

      Being a suddenly woken up Zombie among sleeping Zombies can cause life-changing problems for you.

      (Not for the sleeping Zombies, however, as long as they can stay asleep.)

      But what then? You are alone? You are outcast? Perhaps you are perceived as 'the enemy'? This could get 'bad'.

      Perhaps you should consider what might happen before you jump into irreversible awareness expansion...

      WHAT YOU NOTICE

      One of

      WHAT OTHERS DO NOT NOTICE

      CREATING WITH THE DIFFERENCE BETWEEN WHAT YOU NOTICE AND WHAT OTHERS DO NOT NOTICE

      This difference between what you notice that you notice, and what you notice that other people do not notice, may not, at first, be very large.

       

      On the other hand, the very fact that you are noticing that you notice things that other people are not noticing can be devastatingly disillusioning. The differences in noticing between you and someone else may be your first irrefutable evidence that you are indeed different from other people... or that they are different from you... depending upon if you are self-referenced or other-referenced.

       

      'Self-Referenced' means that your framework for interacting with reality is your own Authority (with a capital 'A', implying that your Authority is inalienable. It came with you being born.)

       

      'Other-Referenced' means that your framework for interacting with reality is other people's authority (with no capital 'a', implying that their 'authority' is circumstantially granted to them by their position in some kind of organizational hierarchy, or from the certificate hanging on their wall).

       

      After noticing that you can notice differences between human beings so startling as that you notice things that they do not notice, there are some different things you can do with this. The question being, are you doing these things consciously for the benefit of all beings everywhere, or are you doing these things neurotically for the benefit of the Gremlin food you can create?

       

      Neurotic Self-Serving Gremlin Things:

      1. Use the experience that you notice something that someone else does not to insult their intelligence, run circles around them, abuse them, 
      2. Adopt and enforce the status position that you are superior to them, that they are a stupid brute not to notice what you notice.
      3. Secretly arrange things so that your inside knowledge gives you an advantage in a certain situation so that you can 'win' and the others can 'lose'.
      Collaborative Transformational Things:
      1. Use the experience that you notice something that someone else does not to create clarity and possibilities for the other person so that by sharing your Treasure, the Treasure itself grows, and more Winning is Happening.
      2. Create, hold, and Navigate 3 Phase Healing or Transformational Processes or Initiations for others to they can move along their Path.
      3. Offer coaching, possibility making, or negotiation training or services for others or for your team.
      4. Provide feedback and coaching about how to notice more than what a person or organization is currently noticing, such as the possibility of entering new market space.
      5. Create Legends about how you became able to notice or distinguish things that other people cannot notice or distinguish, and use the effects of your Legends to guide people who are attracted into optimizing new potentials for themselves, in terms of their relationships, their health, their life plans.

      Since your energy follows your attention, then whatever has your attention also consumes your energy.
